Apply for outside employment

ON THIS PAGE

    If you want to work outside the Northern Territory Public Sector (NTPS), you must get written approval first.

    Outside employment is work or a service you are doing outside of your government job.

    It applies when you are paid a:

    • salary
    • commission
    • allowance or another reward.

    Read employment instruction 12 - code of conduct on the Office of the Commissioner for Public Employment website.

    What does it include

    Outside employment can include:

    • casual employment
    • working in family business
    • hobbies which may generate an income
    • self-employment
    • content creation or influencer activities on digital platforms that may generate income or benefits
    • consulting activities.

    When to apply

    You must get approval before you start outside work.

    There must not be any conflict of interest between your responsibilities as a government employee and external employment.

    Volunteering

    You don't need to apply for outside employment if you are an unpaid volunteer.

    If an actual or perceived conflict of interest may apply, you must declare the conflict.

    Find out more about conflict of interest and how to declare.

    Employment while on leave

    While you are on paid leave, you can't engage in outside employment.

    If you want to work during your unpaid leave, you must apply before starting the outside employment.

    Before you apply

    Talk to your manager before you commit to outside opportunities or volunteer work.

    It is important you understand what is expected of you.

    You must submit a separate application for each employer.

    Renewal

    Each outside employment application is for a specific period. It is not ongoing.

    If you wish to continue outside employment beyond the date approved, you must submit a new application.

    How to apply

    If you are planning to engage in outside employment, you must fill in and submit the outside employment application.

    If you don't know your outside employment hours, enter the details you do know in the “provide any other details about outside employment” comment box under the ‘Employment details’ tab.

    Applications that do not contain sufficient information may not be approved by the delegate.

    For assistance, contact your manager or Workforce Services.

    Apply now

    Manage your application

    You can save your progress on an un-submitted application by clicking the save icon.

    You must name the application the first time you save.

    A link will be emailed to you so you can return at any time.

    To keep track of forms in progress, go to myForms.

    After you apply

    After you submit your application, it will be sent to your manager for review.

    You will receive updates as your application progresses through the approval chain.

    Once your application has been completed by your agency delegate, a copy will be emailed to you for your records.

    If you have attached a conflict of interest declaration, you must also provide a PDF copy of your application to your agency governance and risk unit.

    If your application is approved

    All outside employment is to be done in your private time outside of your government work hours.

    You can't list or use your government contact details for outside employment purposes.

    You also can't use any government property, facilities, equipment or services.

    This includes computers, email, printers and paper and stationery.

    If your application is not approved

    You must not engage in outside employment.

    If you do, you may be in breach of your employment conditions.

    Your terms and conditions of employment are set out in the code of conduct.

    If your outside employment changes

    You will need to re-apply if your government or outside employment role changes.

    The changes can be any of the below:

    • changes to the days or hours worked
    • increase or decrease in the total hours worked
    • starting work with a new or 2nd job or activity
    • any changes to your government employment or position.
